# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Project Telos serves people handing consequential work to AI systems: AI workflow operators, agent-loop builders, engineers, research and editorial teams, clinical-adjacent reviewers, studios, and due-diligence users who need a record they can inspect later. They are usually in an active workflow, not browsing a marketing page: they need to see what was gathered, what was checked, what drifted, what could not be verified, and what should happen next.

## Product Purpose

Project Telos gives a stateless model durable, verified contact with state and range. It binds intake, structure, routing, verification, and reconciliation into a re-checkable operator surface across the five flagship tools: Gather, Crucible, Index, Forum, and Telos. Success means a user or host system can move from action to action with provenance attached, understand the current evidence state, and prefer an honest `UNVERIFIABLE` over a fluent unsupported answer.

## Brand Personality

Calm, exacting, and humane. The voice should feel rigorous without becoming hostile, clear without flattening the ambition, and sober enough for serious AI, scientific, medical, research, and operational workflows.

## Anti-references

Do not make this feel like a generic SaaS landing page, a decorative AI dashboard, a marketing hero, or a model self-critique wrapper that only restates confidence. Avoid gratuitous cards, inflated metrics, decorative gradients, invented controls, and any surface that hides uncertainty. The interface should not imply evidence exists when the tools cannot re-derive it.

## Design Principles

- Practice the thesis: every confident state should be backed by a visible check, receipt, or re-run path.
- Make uncertainty useful: `UNVERIFIABLE` is an actionable state, not an error to bury.
- Keep the tools standalone and composable: CLI, MCP, plugins, IDEs, TUIs, and app surfaces should share the same contracts.
- Optimize for operators in flow: show status, next action, and evidence before explanation.
- Let rigor feel approachable: the system can be dense and technical without being cryptic.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use WCAG AA as the floor for contrast and keyboard access. Preserve reduced-motion behavior, avoid color-only status encoding, keep dense data scannable with text labels, and make current state, failure state, and next action available to screen readers and non-visual host surfaces.
