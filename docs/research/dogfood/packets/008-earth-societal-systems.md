Status: `SOURCE_LEAD` plus `HIGH_RISK_HYPOTHESIS`

## Question

Can Telos help with climate, energy, epidemiology, infrastructure, and policy decisions by making model claims and decision evidence inspectable?

## Working Thesis

Societal-scale systems need proof packets because decisions rely on models, datasets, assumptions, uncertainty, forecasts, policies, and human consequences.

Confidence: moderate for need; low for any decision recommendation until data and domain review exist.

## Packet Requirement

A decision packet must include:

- data provenance;
- model version and training/evaluation boundary;
- forecast or simulation output;
- uncertainty and calibration;
- decision threshold;
- impacted population or asset class;
- intervention/action record;
- review and dissent notes;
- post-action measurement plan.

## Adversarial Steelman

Objection: societal decision packets can create false confidence if they compress uncertainty into a clean report.

Response: accepted. Telos must foreground uncertainty and dissent. For these domains, `UNVERIFIABLE` is often the correct verdict.

## Next Proof Attempt

Start with a public weather forecast comparison packet: model forecast, observed weather, error metric, uncertainty, and decision threshold. Do not generalize beyond the measured window.
